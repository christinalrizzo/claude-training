Connect Salesforce to Claude

In this chapter you'll add the Salesforce connection inside Claude's Cowork mode and authorize it to access your Pelago Salesforce org. This only needs to be done once — after setup, the connection persists across sessions.

The Salesforce integration lives inside Claude's Cowork mode — the desktop app, not the browser version at claude.ai. Make sure you have the Claude desktop app installed and are signed in with your Pelago account.

💡
If you don't have the Claude desktop app yet, download it from claude.ai/download. Sign in with your @pelagohealth.com email.
⚠️
The Salesforce connection is only available in the desktop app with Cowork mode. It won't appear in the browser version of Claude.

Cowork mode is Claude's workspace for managing files and automated tasks. Connectors — like Salesforce — live here and give Claude access to your external tools and data.

  1. Open the Claude desktop app
  2. Look for the Cowork button or tab in the sidebar or home screen
  3. Click it to start a Cowork session — you'll land in a chat interface with access to your connectors
✅
You'll know you're in Cowork mode when you see the workspace folder panel and the connectors panel along the side of the chat interface.

Connections (also called connectors) are what give Claude access to external services like Salesforce. Adding the Salesforce connection lets Claude query your Pelago org directly from the chat.

  1. In your Cowork session, look for the connectors panel (usually on the left sidebar or accessible via a plug/link icon)
  2. Search for "Salesforce"
  3. Click Add or Connect next to the Salesforce connector
  4. Wait a moment — this usually takes just a few seconds
💡
If you already see Salesforce listed as a connected connector, you can skip ahead to the next step.

Once the Salesforce connector is added, you need to authorize it with your Pelago Salesforce account. This is a one-time step using OAuth — the same login flow you'd use for any other Salesforce integration.

  1. Click Connect or Authorize next to the Salesforce connector
  2. A Salesforce login window will open in your browser
  3. Sign in with your Pelago Salesforce credentials (your @pelagohealth.com email)
  4. Click Allow when prompted to grant Claude access to your Salesforce data
  5. Return to Claude — you should see a green "Connected" status next to the Salesforce connector
⚠️
Make sure you're logging into Pelago's production Salesforce org, not a sandbox. If you're unsure which org to use, check with Christina before authorizing.
✅
Claude accesses Salesforce using your own credentials, so it will only be able to see and do what you personally have permission to in Salesforce. No extra permissions are granted.
2

Verify & Understand Your Access

Before diving into real queries, let's confirm the connection is working and make sure you understand what Claude can — and can't — do with your Salesforce data.

The fastest way to confirm your connection is working is to ask Claude a simple question that requires it to access Salesforce. Try one of the prompts below — if Claude returns real data from your org, you're all set.

Try this prompt
Who am I connected to in Salesforce? Show me my user info.

Or if you want to test with real records:

Show me the 5 most recently modified accounts in Salesforce.
✅
If Claude returns your name, email, and Salesforce user ID — or a list of accounts — the connection is confirmed and working correctly.
⚠️
If Claude says it can't access Salesforce or returns an error, go back to Step 4 and re-authorize. If the problem persists, use the ? button at the bottom of this page to message Christina.

Claude's Salesforce connection gives it the ability to look up and edit records in your org — but it has important limitations. Read this carefully before you start using it with real data.

✅
Claude CAN: Look up Accounts, Contacts, and Opportunities. Search and summarize data across multiple records. Edit field values on existing records — for example, adding or removing a partner on an opportunity, updating a stage, or clearing a field value.
⚠️
Claude CANNOT create new records. It cannot create a new Account, Opportunity, or Contact — even if you ask it to. If you need to create a record, do it directly in Salesforce.
⚠️
Claude CANNOT delete records. It cannot delete an Account, Opportunity, or Contact as a whole. However, it can clear or remove information on a record — for example, removing a partner from an opportunity or blanking out a field value.
💡
Claude works best when you give it specific context — like an account name, opportunity name, or date range. The more specific your question, the more useful the answer.

Try It Out

Now that you're connected, try at least 2 of these 3 practice queries in your Claude session. Each one demonstrates a different type of thing you can do with the Salesforce integration. Click a card to see instructions and a ready-to-use prompt.

What this does

Ask Claude to retrieve details about a specific account — including key contacts, open opportunities, and any recent activity. Great for quick pre-call research.

How to use it

  1. Copy the prompt below (click it)
  2. Replace [Account Name] with a real account name from Pelago's Salesforce org
  3. Paste it into your Claude Cowork chat and hit Enter

Prompt

Look up the account [Account Name] in Salesforce and give me a quick summary — key contacts, any open opportunities, and the current account status.
💡
Tip: You don't need to know the exact account name — Claude can search by partial name too. Try "Find accounts with 'Health' in the name" if you're not sure what to look up.

What this does

Ask Claude to pull your open opportunities and summarize the pipeline — by stage, close date, or value. Useful for pipeline reviews and forecasting prep without digging through Salesforce reports.

How to use it

  1. Copy the prompt below
  2. Paste it into your Claude Cowork chat
  3. Review the output — Claude will show you a structured view of your pipeline

Prompt

Show me my open opportunities in Salesforce. Summarize them by stage and highlight any that are closing within the next 30 days.
💡
Tip: You can ask Claude to filter further — e.g. "Show me only opportunities over $50K" or "Which of these are stuck in the same stage for more than 60 days?"

What this does

Look up a specific contact by name or company, and have Claude summarize their role, account relationship, and any recent opportunities they're involved in. Useful before outreach or meetings.

How to use it

  1. Copy the prompt below
  2. Replace [Contact Name or Company] with a real person or organization
  3. Paste it into Claude and review the response

Prompt

Find the contact [Contact Name or Company] in Salesforce. What account are they linked to, what's their title, and are there any open opportunities associated with them?
💡
Tip: If you don't have a specific name in mind, try "Find all contacts at [Company Name]" to browse who's in the org from a specific account.
