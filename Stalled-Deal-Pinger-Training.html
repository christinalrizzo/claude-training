Enable the plugin, authorize, and verify the connection. Takes about 3 minutes.

The Stalled Deal Pinger

How the automated two-part workflow keeps deals moving with almost no effort from you.

Part 1 — Morning (Around 8am MDT): Claude queries Salesforce for any of your deals that are in SAO stage or later, closing in 2026, and haven't been recently updated. If it finds stalled deals, it sends you a Slack DM listing them with their details.

Part 2 — Afternoon (Around 4pm MDT): Claude reads the replies in each Slack thread, interprets what you wrote, and automatically updates the Next Step and Stage fields in Salesforce. It then sends Christina a summary of all changes made that day.

Nothing to set up. This workflow is already running. You'll start receiving pings automatically as long as your Salesforce connection is active.

Claude looks for deals that meet all three of these criteria:

  • Stage: SAO (Sales Accepted Opportunity) or any later stage
  • Close date: Closing in 2026
  • Activity: No logged activity in 14+ days — "last touch" means the last time an email, call, or note was logged on the opportunity record in Salesforce
Early-stage deals won't be pinged. Deals in Discovery or earlier stages are not included. Only deals that are actively progressing (SAO+) and showing no movement get flagged.

If you have a deal that looks stalled but you've been actively working it, make sure you're logging emails, calls, or notes on the opportunity record in Salesforce — that's what resets the clock.

Around Around 8am MDT on weekdays, you'll receive a direct message from Claude in Slack if you have stalled deals. It will look something like this:

No action needed until you're ready. You can reply to the ping anytime before Around 4pm MDT and your updates will be captured. Replies received after Around 4pm MDT will be picked up the following business day.

Reply directly in the Slack thread (not as a new DM — use the thread reply). Write naturally — Claude understands plain English. Just tell it what's happening with each deal.

Claude will pick up on three things from your reply:

  • Next Step — what you're doing next (e.g., "sending a proposal Friday", "following up after their board meeting")
  • Stage change — if you mention moving a deal forward or back (e.g., "move to Negotiation", "they went cold")
  • No action needed — if you let Claude know a deal doesn't need updating

Example replies that work well:

Acme Corp — call scheduled for Friday, I'll have a proposal to them by end of next week. Move to Negotiation. Widget Co — went quiet after the last call, following up Monday. Keep stage as-is, next step: send check-in email.
Acme — they're in budget review, decision expected May 15. Next step: check in May 14. Widget — actually closed lost, pulled out of budget process.
Both of these I've been actively working — update next steps to "following up this week" for both, no stage changes.
Reply in the thread, not as a new message. Click "Reply" on Claude's original ping message so your update is linked to the right deals.
Don't stress about the format. Claude doesn't require a specific template — just be clear about what you want updated. If it's ambiguous, Claude will make its best interpretation and you can see the changes in Salesforce.

Every weekday at Around 4pm MDT, Claude runs a second automated task that:

  1. Reads all the replies that came in from reps since the morning ping
  2. Matches each reply to the correct Salesforce opportunity
  3. Updates the Next Step field with what you said
  4. Changes the Stage if you mentioned one
  5. Sends Christina a summary of all deals updated, what changed, and which reps didn't reply
Your Salesforce record is updated automatically. You don't need to log into Salesforce to record your update — your Slack reply does it for you.
Deadline to reply: Around 4pm MDT same day. Replies received after the afternoon processor runs will be picked up the following business day.

After Around 4pm MDT on any day you replied to a ping, you can ask Claude to verify that your Salesforce updates went through.

Show me the current Next Step and Stage for [opportunity name]
What's the latest on my open opportunities? Show me the Next Step for each one.

You should see the values you described in your Slack reply reflected in Salesforce. If something looks off, you can ask Claude to correct it directly:

Update the Next Step on [opportunity name] to: [your updated text]
Claude will always show you the before and after, and ask for your confirmation before saving any manual update.
